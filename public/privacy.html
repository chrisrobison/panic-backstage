Privacy & Cookie Policy

Last updated 25 June 2026

This policy explains how Panic Backstage handles personal data in the venue management application — the workspace where venues run bookings from inquiry to settlement. It is written to meet our obligations under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the UK GDPR.

1. Controllers, processors, and your account

There are two kinds of personal data in Panic Backstage, with different responsibilities:

  • Your staff account. Panic Backstage is the controller for the personal data of the people who log in to run the workspace (name, email, phone, sign-in credentials, and interface preferences). This policy governs that data.
  • The venue’s own records. When a venue uses the app to manage its audience, leads, guests, and ticket buyers, the venue is the controller of that data and Panic Backstage acts as its processor, handling it only on the venue’s instructions under our data processing terms. If you are a customer of a venue and want to exercise your rights over that data, contact the venue; we will assist them in responding.

3. Cookies and local storage

Panic Backstage authenticates with a token rather than a traditional session cookie, and loads no third-party trackers. Fonts and icons are served from our own servers, so using the app does not disclose your activity to any font or icon CDN.

  • Strictly necessary — no consent required: the sign-in token kept in your browser’s local storage to keep you logged in, a CSRF token that protects requests, and a small record of your cookie choice (pb.cookieConsent).
  • Preferences — consent required: interface settings remembered on your device (for example, whether the navigation rail is collapsed). These are stored only if you choose “Accept all” in the cookie banner, and never if you choose “Essential only”.

You can withdraw consent at any time by choosing “Essential only” or clearing this site’s data in your browser.

4. Service providers we share data with

We share personal data only with providers that help us deliver the service, under written terms and only as needed. Depending on the features a venue uses, these may include:

  • Payments & ticketing: Stripe, Square, Eventbrite, Luma.
  • Email & messaging: SendGrid, Mailchimp.
  • Promotion channels (when used): Facebook/Instagram, TikTok.
  • Accounting: QuickBooks Online, Xero.
  • Contracts & e-signature: Dropbox Sign.
  • AI lead parsing: Anthropic (used to structure forwarded booking emails).
  • Hosting and infrastructure providers that operate our servers.

Some providers are located outside the European Economic Area or the UK. Where personal data is transferred internationally, it is protected by appropriate safeguards such as the European Commission’s Standard Contractual Clauses.

5. How long we keep it

  • Account data is kept while your account is active.
  • On an erasure request, your personal data is anonymised or deleted within 30 days; residual copies in encrypted backups are purged on the normal rotation, within 90 days.
  • Financial and ticketing records (orders, payments, settlements) are retained for up to 7 years to meet tax and accounting obligations, stripped of identifying buyer details where the person has been erased.
  • Sign-in, refresh, magic-link, and email-verification tokens expire automatically per their short time-to-live.

6. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to access, rectify, erase, restrict, object to processing, and port your data, to withdraw consent where we rely on it, and to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.

Self-service tools

While signed in you can exercise the two most common rights directly:

  • Download your data (access & portability): request GET /api/account/export while signed in to download a JSON copy of your account data.
  • Erase your account (right to be forgotten): send POST /api/account/delete with {"confirm": true, "email": "your email"}. This anonymises your account and removes your credentials; financial records required by law are retained without your identifying details.

You can also exercise any right — or ask us to act for a venue whose data you are a subject of — by emailing tom@themab.org. We respond within one month.

7. How we protect your data

Passwords are stored only as hashes and passkeys use public-key credentials, so we never hold a usable copy of your secret. Stored third-party credentials are encrypted, requests are protected against cross-site request forgery, and access to personal data is limited to what each role needs.

8. Changes to this policy

If we change how we handle personal data we will update this page and the “last updated” date above, and where appropriate ask you to review the updated policy in the app.
